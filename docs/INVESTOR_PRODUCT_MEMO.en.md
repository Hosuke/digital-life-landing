# Investor Product Memo (Digital Life)

## 1. The Investor Questions We Must Answer
- Is this a durable business or a one-off demo?
- Will users pay, and will they stay after paying?
- Can unit economics improve with scale?

## 2. Our Thesis (Testable)
- Business form: long-term companion service inside IM apps, not single delivery.
- Monetization logic: low-friction trial acquisition + emotional Aha + threshold upsell.
- Unit economics: model-tier routing, asset inventory reuse, and trial caps to control COGS.

## 3. Target Users and Market Entry
- Early adopters: families with emotional memorial/companionship needs and high AI acceptance.
- Entry scenarios: memorial companionship, family emotional support, growth-memory dialog.
- Why now: multimodal model maturity + stable IM channels + declining generation costs.

## 4. Product Stack
- Front stage: sci-fi narrative landing for conversion and emotional framing.
- Control plane: UID-driven orchestration and full-funnel state traceability.
- Runtime: Yaya persona engine for response and media generation.
- Delivery surface: Telegram/WhatsApp.

## 5. Growth and Conversion Flywheel
1. Content-led acquisition -> landing
2. UID deep-link activation -> IM
3. Asset intake + initialization -> Aha moment
4. Threshold gating + urgency prompt -> paid upgrade
5. Subscription + add-ons -> higher ARPU and retention
6. Word-of-mouth -> lower CAC over time

## 6. Revenue Model
- Core revenue: subscription (monthly/quarterly/annual)
- Add-on revenue: multimedia pack, anniversary pack, family-seat expansion
- Mid/long-term: B2B white-label licensing

## 7. Cost Structure
- Variable: model calls (text/image/video), BSP messaging fees
- Fixed: engineering, operations, baseline infrastructure
- Main control levers:
  - trial media generation cap
  - tiered model routing (cheap-first, premium-on-demand)
  - inventory reuse + async processing queues

## 8. Milestones for Investment Decisions
- M1: UID bind rate >= 60%, asset completion >= 40%
- M2: Aha completion >= 50%, trial->paid >= 8-12%
- M3: D30 retention >= 20%, LTV/CAC > 2
- M4: at least one repeatable acquisition channel with stable CAC

## 9. Risks and Mitigations
- Quality volatility -> fallback chain and explainable failure messaging
- Compliance/privacy -> data minimization, auditability, deletion lifecycle
- Cost spikes -> dynamic rate limits, model substitution, vendor negotiation

## 10. Investment Recommendation Framework
If M2 is reached within 8-12 weeks, the project is investable for scale validation.
If M2 remains below target, narrow to high-intent verticals or B2B deployment to reduce C-end marketing burn.
